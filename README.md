# Fusion Flavours: A Recipe Book for Cultural Exploration

A student-focused digital cookbook developed as a full end-to-end project management exercise, covering project initiation, planning, execution, monitoring, and closure. The project was completed as part of the **Project Life Cycle Management and Communications** course at **St. Lawrence College, School of Business (Business Analytics program)** and received an **A+ grade**.

---

## Project Overview

Fusion Flavours is a digital cookbook designed for college students seeking affordable, culturally diverse, and beginner-friendly recipes. The project combined structured project management methodology with real deliverable production — resulting in a 33-page cookbook, a feedback survey, and a complete set of project documentation.

---

## Skills Demonstrated

| Skill | Application |
|---|---|
| Project Planning | Defined scope, milestones, timeline, and budget using a structured project charter |
| Data Collection & Analysis | Designed and distributed a Google Forms feedback survey; collected and analyzed 22 responses |
| Stakeholder Management | Conducted a full stakeholder analysis identifying interests, concerns, and contributions |
| Risk Management | Identified 7 project risks with likelihood assessment and mitigation strategies |
| Feasibility Analysis | Compared printed vs. digital delivery options across 5 evaluation criteria |
| Data Cleaning | Anonymized survey response data for responsible public sharing |
| Scheduling & Tracking | Built and maintained a Gantt chart covering 15 parent tasks and 30+ child tasks |
| Digital Tools | Canva, Google Forms, Microsoft Excel, Microsoft Word |

---

## Key Project Facts

- **Timeline:** June 2024 – August 2024
- **Team Size:** 2
- **Recipes Included:** 7 cuisines × 2 versions (traditional + vegetarian) + 5 fusion recipes = 19 recipes total
- **Feedback Collected:** 22 responses via Google Forms
- **Estimated Budget:** $300 (academic planning exercise)
- **Final Grade:** A+

---

## Feedback Highlights

Of the 22 students who responded to the feedback survey:
- The majority said food can connect people from different cultural backgrounds
- Butter Chicken, Momo, and Jollof Rice were the most popular recipes
- Most respondents enjoyed reading the cultural stories behind each recipe
- Butter Chicken Enchiladas and Tacos Momo were the top-rated fusion recipes
